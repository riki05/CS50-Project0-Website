• Perennial autumn flowering herb.
  • A member of the Iridaceae family that includes herbs with rhizomes, corms or bulbs.
  • It has underground fleshy starch storing organs called corms.
  • Leaves are basal, grass-like, dark green with whitish median strip.
  • Flowers typically contain 3 stigmas each which are dried to obtain saffron.
  • Major chemical components in saffron include crocin, crocetin, safranal and picrocrocin which impart color, taste, odor and contribute to its medicinal properties.
  • The quantity of these pigments in dried saffron defines the quality of the product (Lage and Cantrell, 2009).
