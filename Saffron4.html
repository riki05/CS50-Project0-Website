Saffron Diseases and disease management

  • Corm rot disease of saffron is a problem prevalent worldwide in saffron growing regions (Thakur et al., 1992; Cappelli et al., 1991; Cappelli and Di Minco, 1999; Di Primo and Cappelli, 2000; Hassan and Devi, 2003; Kalha et al., 2007).
  • The pathogens reported to cause corm rot disease in saffron include Rhizoctonia crocorum, Phoma crocophila, Fusarium monoliforme var. intermedium, Macrophomina phaseolina, F. oxysporum, F. solani, F. pallidoroseum, F. equiseti, Mucor sp., Penicillium sp. and Sclerotium rolfsii (Nehvi et al., 2006; Husaini et al., 2010).
  • F. oxysporum has been reported to be the most destructive of all the saffron pathogens worldwide.
  • F. oxysporum R1 isolate from Kashmir (India) has been reported to be one of the saffron pathogens in Kashmir region.
  • A bacterial isolate (Bacillus amyloliquefaciens W2) from the same saffron growing region has been found effective in controlling the disease incidence (Gupta and Vakhlu, 2015).
