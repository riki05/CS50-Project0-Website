3.Production and Trade

Saffron, world’s most costly spice, is obtained as dried crimson colored stigma from the flower of an auto-triploid male sterile perennial herb Crocus sativus (Tsimidou and Tarantilis, 2016).

One pound of the spice is priced at US$1000 and takes the collection of about 75,000 flowers (Masi et al., 2016).
The cultivation and collection process is manual and labor intensive (Díez-Méndez and Rivas, 2017).
The spice has been in use since ages for its color, flavor and medicinal properties (Kyriakoudi et al., 2015).

Major producers that contribute to world’s harvest of saffron are Iran, Greece, Morocco, India (J&K) and Spain. It is also being cultivated in Turkey, France, Switzerland, Israel, Pakistan, China, Egypt, United Arab Emirates, Japan, Afghanistan, Iraq and Australia (Tasmania) (Yasmin and Nehvi, 2013; Díez-Méndez and Rivas, 2017).
